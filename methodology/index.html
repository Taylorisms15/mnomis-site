Motivational Intelligence Framework

Interpret behavior to guide outreach

Mnomis interprets engagement behavior to identify how members are most likely to receive outreach at the moment action is required.

Stable motivational tendencies shape engagement

Members differ in how they engage with care. Some prefer autonomy and self-direction. Others respond best to clear structure and guidance. Some rely on trusted relationships, while others act primarily when urgency becomes visible.

These tendencies are relatively stable over time. They influence how outreach is received, whether members follow through, and how receptive they are to engagement.

Behavior reveals underlying engagement posture

Motivational tendencies cannot be directly observed. They are inferred through patterns of engagement behavior accumulated over time.

  • Appointment attendance consistency
  • Care gap completion persistence
  • Channel response patterns
  • Follow-through after escalation events
  • Digital versus human engagement reliance
  • Response latency and avoidance patterns

These signals combine into interpretable engagement signatures that reflect how outreach is most likely to be received.

Context determines what is most active now

Motivational tendencies are stable, but their expression shifts depending on context. A member who typically prefers autonomy may seek structure after an acute event. A member who normally avoids engagement may become receptive when urgency increases.

Mnomis continuously interprets behavioral signals to identify which motivational drivers are most active when outreach occurs.

Interpretation becomes operational guidance

Mnomis translates interpretation into clear Outreach Intelligence Briefs that help care teams decide:

  • Which outreach channel to use
  • What tone and framing to apply
  • When outreach is most likely to succeed
  • Whether escalation or reinforcement is appropriate
  • How confident the interpretation is

Outputs are designed for human interpretation and workflow integration, not automated clinical decision-making.
